4.1 Deformation Of Solids

4.1.1 Hookes Law

Hooke's Law - The restoring force is directly proportional to the extension, provided the limit of proportionality is not exceeded

4.1.2 Elastic Deformation + Plastic Deformation

Elastic Deformation - when the load is removed the material will return to its original length/shape. The material may or may not follow Hooke's law.
Plastic Deformation - The material is extendable but does not follow Hooke's law, any extenstion is permanent.

4.1.3 Limit of Proportionality Vs Elastic limit

The Limit Of Proportionality - The point to which Hooke's law applies.
The Elastic Limit - The point after which the material will be permanently extended when unloading.

4.1.4 Stress, Strain + The Young Modulus

Stress - the amount of force applied to a solid over a given area.
Strain - the ratio of extension to original length.

4.2 Thermal Physics

4.2.1 Gas Laws

Boyle's Law - for a fixed mass of gas at constant temperature ,the volume is inversely proportional to the applied pressure.
Charles Law - for a fixed mass of gas at constant pressure ,the volume is directly proportional to the Kelvin temperature.
Gay-Lussac Law - for a fixed mass of gas at constant volume ,the pressure is directly proportional to the Kelvin temperature.

4.2.4 Internal Energy

Internal Energy - For a real gas The sum of the potential and kinetic energy for it's molecules.
Internal Energy - For an ideal gas The sum of the kinetic energy for it's molecules.

4.2.7 Absolute Zero

Absolute Zero - The temperature at which all molecular motion stops approx -273.16°C (for calculations -273°C is suffiecient).

4.2.8 Specific Heat Capicity

Specific Heat Capacity - The quantity of heat energy needed to rise the tempeture of 1kg of the material by 1k.

4.3 Uniform Circular Motion

4.3.1 Angular Velocity

Angular Velocity - The angle turned through over the time taken.

4.4 Simple Harmonic Motion

Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM) - If an object's acceleration is directly proportional to its displacement from its rest position and is always directed towards that position then it is undergoing simple harmonic motion.
